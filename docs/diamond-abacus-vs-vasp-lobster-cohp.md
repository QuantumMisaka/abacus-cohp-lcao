## Qualitative COHP Agreement

The chemically robust comparison is made on the sign and energy placement of `-COHP/-pCOHP`, not on direct equality of absolute ICOHP values. Under this plotting convention, positive occupied area indicates bonding contribution and negative unoccupied area indicates antibonding contribution.

| Curve | Occupied integral | Occupied max and energy | Occupied min and energy | Unoccupied min and energy |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| ABACUS C(sp)-C(sp) | 0.085500 | 0.020587 at -18.078 eV | -0.000035 at -4.328 eV | -0.455879 at 11.072 eV |
| ABACUS C(all)-C(all) | 0.057463 | 0.021061 at -18.078 eV | -0.003814 at -6.628 eV | -0.457263 at 11.072 eV |
| LOBSTER Bunge | 9.589433 | 1.436429 at -11.128 eV | 0.000000 at -22.028 eV | -2.353550 at 14.172 eV |

Same qualitative information:

- Both workflows identify the occupied nearest-neighbor C-C states as net bonding: all occupied integrals of `-COHP/-pCOHP` are positive.
- Both workflows place the main bonding information below the Fermi level and show little bonding weight right at E_F, consistent with the insulating diamond electronic structure.
- Both workflows show antibonding character above E_F: LOBSTER has a broad negative unoccupied `-pCOHP` region, and ABACUS also has clear negative `-COHP` features in the conduction-band energy range.

Differences that matter chemically:

- Absolute scale differs strongly. LOBSTER reports `-ICOHP = 9.589420` for one nearest-neighbor C-C interaction, while the current ABACUS postprocessor gives `0.085500` for C(sp)-C(sp) and `0.057463` for C(all)-C(all). This should be treated as a projection/normalization difference, not as a weaker C-C bond in ABACUS.
- LOBSTER's occupied `-pCOHP` is positive through essentially the whole occupied window. ABACUS C(sp)-C(sp) is also mostly positive but becomes nearly zero, with only a tiny negative excursion, near the valence-band top.
- ABACUS C(all)-C(all) includes d-like polarization functions and therefore gives more negative weight in the upper occupied region than the sp-only channel. This makes the all-NAO integral smaller than the sp-sp integral.
- The unoccupied antibonding distribution differs: LOBSTER shows broad negative `-pCOHP` from about 4 to 18 eV, while ABACUS has its strongest negative feature near 8 to 12 eV and additional basis-dependent structure above 12 eV.

## Scientific Interpretation

- ABACUS primary comparison uses the precision-basis C(sp)-C(sp) channel to mirror LOBSTER `includeOrbitals sp`.
- ABACUS all-NAO C-C is reported separately because d-like polarization functions can change the integrated bonding strength.
- The three curves are aligned to their own Fermi levels and shown as `-COHP` or `-pCOHP`, so larger positive occupied area indicates stronger bonding.
- LOBSTER gives a much larger absolute -ICOHP scale than the current ABACUS NAO COHP post-processor. This is expected for the present implementation because ABACUS and LOBSTER use different projection/basis definitions and normalization conventions.
- The robust comparison is therefore the sign, energy location, and qualitative bonding/antibonding shape of the occupied C-C states, not one-to-one equality of ICOHP values.
